## 2. The Nature of the Service — Read This First

**Sharky displays an Environmental Conditions Index ("the Index"), a numerical score (0–100) and accompanying level (NEUTRAL / ELEVATED / HIGH / SEVERE) that reflects the likelihood of bull shark presence in nearshore marine environments based on publicly available environmental data.**

**The Index is an environmental observation tool only. It is not:**

- a guarantee of safety or danger at any beach;

**You enter the water entirely at your own risk.** Always follow the instructions of surf lifesavers, beach flags, and official signage. In an emergency, call **000**.

## 5. The Environmental Conditions Index — Scope and Limitations

### 5.1 Data Sources

The Index is computed using data obtained from third-party sources including, but not limited to:

- **Bureau of Meteorology (BOM)** — rainfall, river discharge, and tidal data;
- **NSW Department of Primary Industries / SharkSmart** — shark sighting and detection reports sourced from the SharkSmart NSW public data feed; and
- **NASA/JPL NOAA MUR Sea Surface Temperature** dataset via NOAA CoastWatch ERDDAP.

We do not control these sources and cannot guarantee their accuracy, completeness, or timeliness. Data may be delayed by several hours or unavailable due to station outages, transmission failures, or third-party service interruptions.

### 5.2 Coverage Limitations

The Service covers selected beaches in New South Wales, Australia. Not all beaches are covered. The absence of a beach from the Service does not imply that location is safe or shark-free. Index scores are calibrated for **bull shark** activity; other species may be present and are not fully represented in the model.

### 5.3 Model Limitations

The scoring model is based on published peer-reviewed environmental research. It is a probabilistic environmental model, not a deterministic prediction of shark presence. Environmental signals do not determine shark behaviour with certainty. A NEUTRAL score does not mean no sharks are present, and a SEVERE score does not mean a shark incident will occur.

### 5.4 Update Frequency

Environmental data is updated approximately every hour via automated processes. Sighting data from NSW SharkSmart is updated approximately every 15 minutes. Scores may not reflect events that occurred within the most recent update cycle.

### 5.5 Data Confidence Tiers

Some beaches are displayed with reduced data confidence (LIMITED or MONITORING tiers) where fewer environmental signals are available. Scores for these beaches carry greater uncertainty and should be interpreted with additional caution.

## 6. Sightings Data

The Service displays shark sighting and detection information sourced from the SharkSmart NSW public data feed, operated by the NSW Department of Primary Industries and Regional Development. This includes visual sightings reported by DPI officers and surf lifesaving patrols, SMART drumline alerts, and detections of electronically tagged sharks at receiver stations.

Sightings displayed in the Service are provided for informational purposes only. We do not independently verify this data. Users cannot submit sighting reports through the Service.

**The sightings display is not an emergency service.** If you witness a shark attack or believe someone is in immediate danger, call **000** immediately.
